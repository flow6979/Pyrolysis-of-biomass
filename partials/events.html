Process Flow :

Pyrolysis is a thermochemical process that involves the decomposition of organic materials, such as biomass, in the absence of oxygen. The process typically results in the production of biochar, bio-oil, and syngas. Here is a simplified process flow for the pyrolysis of biomass:

Feedstock Preparation
Biomass feedstock, such as wood chips, agricultural residues, or other organic materials, is collected and prepared for the pyrolysis process. This may involve shredding or chipping the biomass to a suitable size.
Feedstock Drying
Biomass often contains moisture, and it's essential to reduce this moisture content to improve the efficiency of pyrolysis. Drying is typically done before the actual pyrolysis process begins.
Pyrolysis Reactor
The dried biomass is fed into a pyrolysis reactor, which can be a fixed-bed, fluidized bed, or rotary kiln reactor. The reactor is designed to operate in the absence of oxygen to prevent combustion.
Heating and Decomposition
The biomass undergoes heating in the absence of oxygen, leading to thermal decomposition. This process breaks down the complex organic compounds in the biomass into simpler molecules.
Vaporization
As the temperature increases, volatile organic compounds are released from the biomass in the form of vapors. These vapors contain components such as water, organic acids, and gases.
Condensation of Bio-Oil
The vapors are then cooled, leading to the condensation of bio-oil. Bio-oil is a dark liquid that contains a variety of organic compounds. It can be further processed and refined for various applications.
Collection of Syngas
Syngas, a mixture of carbon monoxide, hydrogen, and other gases, is also produced during pyrolysis. This syngas can be collected and used as a fuel or for other applications.
Formation of Biochar
The solid residue left after the pyrolysis process is biochar. This carbon-rich material can be used as a soil amendment to improve soil fertility and sequester carbon.
Product Storage and Utilization
The biochar, bio-oil, and syngas can be stored or further processed for various applications. Biochar can be used in agriculture, bio-oil can be refined for fuel, and syngas can be used for heat and power generation.
